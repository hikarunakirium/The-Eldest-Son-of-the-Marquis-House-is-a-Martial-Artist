Chapter 57: Pimera Mountain

It was still early evening, so after leaving the workshop, I went straight to find the Marquis.

"Yes. What is it?"

The Marquis looked quite busy handling piled-up work. On his desk was a stack of documents about three books high.

"I'd like to go out for a while."

"Go out? Well, you were stuck in the royal palace, so it must have been stuffy. Fine, go ahead. But where are you going?"

The Marquis didn't even look up, head buried in his desk as he gave permission.

"I'm going to Mount Pimera."

"Ah, Mount Pimera. Okay, have a safe— Huh?"

The Marquis's head shot up like lightning, causing the documents on his desk to scatter onto the floor.

"Where did you say you're going?"

"Mount Pimera."

The Marquis finally showed the reaction I expected.

"Why are you suddenly going there? There's nothing to see, and no humans either."

"Yes, I know. But I think I need to go there to make a weapon with the horn I received. My craftsman said that horn isn't ordinary."

"That strange horn?"

The Marquis still thought the fang was nothing special, so he stared at me with a dumbfounded face.

"Hmm, even if you go to Mount Pimera, will the dwarves help you? They don't move easily."

"My craftsman Giranox's hometown is there. So there shouldn't be any problem."

"Your craftsman is human."

"Yes. There's a bit of a story, but anyway, it should be fine. There's nothing particularly dangerous in that area either."

"Well, it is the Holy Kingdom's land…"

There's nothing dangerous there, but something will come from outside.

I mentioned twice — once in front of the king and once in front of the duke — that I would make a weapon with the horn.

Quite a few people heard it, so it must have reached Veil's ears. Veil will definitely prepare something, so I need to move before him.

"If I don't allow it, you'll just sneak out like last time, right?"

"Haha, no way."

The Marquis was referring to the time I pretended to go nearby but actually went to As Castle.

"Sigh, allowing it is better than you running away. Take Arin with you."

"Understood. Thank you."

"Be careful."

"Um…"

"Why?"

"Nothing. Then I'll be going."

"How dull."

I almost told the Marquis to be careful of Veil, but stopped. That would require explaining many things, and right now there was no way to explain.

In the end, I'll have to deal with Veil myself.

"It won't be easy…"

**

In a room melted into complete darkness, only a pair of eyes burned fiercely.

Thud!

A man knelt before the man with burning eyes and slammed his head onto the stone floor.

"Please give me one more chance! I couldn't do anything. Please!"

Thud!

The man banging his head on the floor until it bled was Dar, who had lost to Yuren and been humiliated in front of many people.

Thud!

In front of Dar, the one silently watching with terrifyingly hot eyes was Veil Fabien.

"The moment you failed to secure the fang in the treasury, your value ended."

Unlike his usual gentle voice, Veil's throat produced a beast-like growl.

"Plus, you even kicked away the opportunity that idiot Yuren Rox practically handed you — in just three seconds. Right now, tearing out your throat seems like the better choice. Is there any reason I should give you another chance?"

At Veil's grotesque voice, Dar felt cold sweat pouring madly down his entire body.

"That's why! I fell for his provocation. Since I've been hit once, it won't happen again. I'll create a favorable long-range situation and definitely cut off his breath. Please! Just one more chance!"

Thud!

Dar slammed his already torn forehead onto the floor again. His forehead was ripped open so badly that bone was visible.

"…"

After a long silence, Dar had never felt such terrifying fear since he was born. The extreme pain in his forehead faded in front of Veil's terror.

"Mount Pimera, Mount Saji, Porn Hill, Pokate."

At the place names coming from Veil's mouth, Dar raised his head as if entranced. This was a test. If he didn't know, his neck would be torn off by Veil right here.

Dar desperately spun his mind while looking at the blood-soaked red hand slowly reaching toward him. Thinking about the common point of the four regions, Yuren, and the fang, one answer came out.

"D-dwarves?"

The blood-red hand reaching for Dar stopped.

"Y-Yuren is going to the place with dwarves who can refine the fang!"

Whether it was the correct answer or not, the grim reaper-like red hand returned to Veil. After a moment of silence, Veil spoke.

"I've already sent wolves to each region. If Yuren arrives there, I'll get immediate contact."

The growling disappeared from Veil's voice, returning to his usual calm tone.

"Ah. Yes!"

"I'll give you twenty wolves. This is your last chance."

"Understood. Thank you!"

Thud!

Dar slammed his already torn forehead onto the floor again.

"Get out."

"Thank you so much."

Dar kept bowing his head and left while stroking his neck.

"…"

Even after Dar left, Veil sat motionless for a long time.

"How long are you going to stay like that? Come out."

"Hehe!"

From the pitch-black darkness, a boy who looked like a young teenager popped out with an innocent laugh.

"Ai! If you knew, you should have said so! How embarrassing!"

At the boy's light voice and laughter, Veil frowned.

"Your hobby is still hard to understand. What are you doing at your age?"

"It's no different from you ripping out human spines while they're alive. It's just a hobby."

"So what's the matter?"

"What do you mean 'what's the matter'? I came to help my business partner. We have to finish before they get suspicious."

Veil listened silently to the boy's words.

"Yuren Rox is currently the most famous person in the kingdom. We can't end it with him going missing. There will be problems."

Veil seemed to agree as he quietly listened.

"Anyway, you were planning to kill the trash that just left, right? With my ability, I can make Yuren and that trash kill each other in front of people. I can create the perfect situation where no one suspects anything!"

"What do you want?"

Veil closed his eyes, organized his thoughts for a moment, then spoke quietly again.

"What would a business partner ask for? Help me rise to the position you will reach. Mutual benefit."

Veil nodded slightly as if he had no choice.

"Excellent choice. With my ability, I'll make Yuren and that trash kill each other cleanly. It'll end neatly. Hehehe."

The boy's laughter echoed hollowly in Veil's room as he left.

The next day

Since it was still early evening, after leaving the workshop I went straight to find the Marquis.

Because all schedules at the royal palace were finished and I had received everything, we returned to the marquisate.

I left the marquisate with Arin and Giranox and immediately moved to the Magic Tower.

"Giranox, how did you get from Mount Pimera to As Castle?"

"When I came down the mountain, my master gave me a gem. I used it to move to As Castle and then settled there."

"Warp?"

"No. I walked."

"Walked? How long did it take?"

"About half a year. I also moved slowly on purpose."

Half a year to travel — for someone from the modern world, it was a truly horrible thing to hear. Without the Magic Tower's warp, this would have been a really tiring world.

While listening to Giranox's boring travel story, we warped to Beharun Castle, the Magic Tower closest to Mount Pimera.

"Hmm…"

The moment we stepped out of the Magic Tower in Beharun Castle, it was clear we had entered another country.

"It's insanely big."

The streets were cleaner and more orderly than the royal capital of the kingdom or the imperial capital, but my eyes naturally moved to the massive goddess statue in the center of the town.

"As expected of the Holy Kingdom."

Since Beharun Castle belonged to the Holy Kingdom Iokal, the religious nation's colors were very strong. The people also wore neat clothes and had tidy hair.

"Have you been here before?"

"Yes. I actually thought about settling here, but since it's a religious country, there were many tiring things."

I could understand what Giranox meant. Iokal is a Holy Kingdom, so there were many things you couldn't do and many things you had to do.

"I don't have any particular religion, so it was a bit hard for me to stay in the Holy Kingdom. There was a lot of interference."

"That makes sense."

"That's why I went to the free Cransis Kingdom. I grew up in a dwarf village and didn't belong to any country."

While talking about the Holy Kingdom and Cransis Kingdom, we bought horses and headed straight to Mount Pimera.

"Now that I think about it."

While moving, I realized I still hadn't introduced Arin and Giranox to each other.

"Arin."

"Yes."

"The thin sword I gave you. This friend made it."

"Ah!"

"Giranox, you knew the sword Arin carries is one you made, right?"

"Ah, yes. Of course…"

Giranox's face turned red with embarrassment as he nodded.

"You're so young yet you made such an excellent sword. Your skill is amazing."

"Ah, no. It's because the knight is special. I didn't do anything great."

"Thank you for making such a good sword. I'm using it very well."

"Hah, oh my!"

When Arin slightly bowed her head, Giranox immediately got off his horse and gave her a deep bow.

"Whenever you come, I'll repair it perfectly. Thank you for using my sword."

"Thank you."

"No, I should thank you."

Seeing the two of them politely greeting each other made me smile naturally.

**

"We finally reached Mount Pimera."

It took four days of riding to reach Mount Pimera.

"Aren't you hot? I'm fine since I deal with fire every day, but…"

"I'm fine, but Arin looks a bit tired."

"I'm okay too."

Sweat beaded on Arin's face like dew. Even though she was lightly dressed, the high temperature seemed quite difficult for her.

"It will get a little hotter the higher we go."

When we reached the middle of the mountain, the temperature had risen enough that even with my Celestial Martial Body's heat and cold resistance, I could feel a slight temperature change.

"Arin, draw that sword and use spirit…"

When I tried to tell Arin a good method, a very small sound reached my ear. It was a faint sound like stepping on and breaking a tree branch.

"Why?"

"Just be quiet for a moment."

I maximized internal energy in my hearing and doubled it with Onslon's Watchtower.

Thud.

"Aim for the core…"

"You have to see it first, you old fool!"

"Stop talking nonsense and focus!"

The sound of something breaking and rough voices like they had metal in their throats whispered.

It was a small sound, but I could clearly tell. This was the sound of dwarves fighting something.

"Giranox, Arin."

"Huh? What's going on?"

"I'll go first. Follow me right away."

"Huh? It'll be hard to find the way…"

"I don't know what it is, but it seems the dwarves are being attacked. Move as fast as you can."

"Huh?"

Paaang!

After saying that, I got off the horse and operated So-ryong Ji-bo to the extreme.

"Young master!"

Giranox called, but I didn't look back and ran straight toward where the sound came from.

Already? Did Veil attack? But I'm going to more than one or two places, so how did he know.

Kwaang!

Kugugung!

The higher I went, the louder the destructive and impact sounds became.

"You bastards!"

"Kuaaaar!"

Boom!

Jumping over a steep hill, I saw dwarves in front of me. And I could tell what was happening to them.

"Golems…"

Three 4-meter-tall lava golems covered in lava were engaged in fierce battle with dwarves who were probably under 150cm tall.

They had already been pushed back once — the steel entrance to the village was horribly destroyed.

"Hmm…"

While thinking about my abilities, I once thought about who my natural enemy would be.

The answer came quickly.

Golems.

Special abilities like poison don't work at all, their exterior is so hard that hidden weapons have difficulty piercing them. Plus, unless the core is destroyed, they regenerate faster than trolls.

I had been quite stumped about how to deal with the diamond golem that would appear later.

"Lava golems. A bit annoying."

Lava golems aren't easy opponents either. Still, to help the dwarves, when I moved forward, I saw a small orb pulsing inside the lava golem's body with my eyes.

"That's…"

The golem's core — the heart that would collapse with just a light touch — was clearly visible to my eyes.

"Ah, natural enemy indeed. I'm the golem's natural enemy."
